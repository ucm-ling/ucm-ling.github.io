What is the linguistics club?

The aim of the linguistics club at the University of California, Merced, is to expose curious students to the field of linguistics and help foster a genuine interest in the study. 

Because linguistics is a diverse and varied field, this academic club is focused on engaging members in the various, specific subfields of linguistics, including but not limited to: applied, computational, anthropological, forensic, historical, and sociolinguistics. Discussions on the aforementioned topics will the be primary mode of most club meetings.

There are many misconceptions surrounding linguistics. One does not have to speak a second language to take away valuable lessons from attending discussions, and we will not be policing grammar— in fact, while attending, you can learn that linguistics often does quite the opposite. Everyone can give and gain from the linguistics club, regardless of background. 

A Brief Introduction to Linguistics

There's a myriad of ways to introduce linguistics. No doubt you've thought about things related to linguistics at some point in your life.

One of the first ways people learn about linguistics is through etymology, or even pronunciation. Have you ever seen a word that you had no clue how to pronounce? 

colo·nel
/ˈkərnl/

That's the IPA transcription for the word 'colonel,' as you can probably tell, and it's also one example of a tool linguists have invented to ease the complicated process of communication across language barriers. After all, who hasn't found a word that's hard to pronounce in English?

Hey, where does that word 'colonel' even come from, and why's it pronounced so weirdly? That sort of question is answered by etymologists, people who study the history of words, or who specialize in etymology— not to be confused with entomology: the study of bugs! 

Linguistics is often seen as an interdisciplinary field, and is sometimes even seen as a just subset of anthropology or cognitive science. At the Linguistics Club, we encourage all views of the study, and explore all avenues it offers. Linguistics can fit to nearly any field of study, from politics to education to biology to even math, mostly because the need to connect and communicate is a universally human thing. Come join us and learn about language— there's something for everybody!
